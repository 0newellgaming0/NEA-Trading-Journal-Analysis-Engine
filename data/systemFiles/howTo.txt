RISK MANAGEMENT DASHBOARD - NEA28V1
© 2026 Newell Trading Group. All rights reserved.

=========================================================

INTRODUCTION

The NEA28V1 Risk Management Dashboard is a structured trading and analysis system designed to help traders identify, evaluate, and manage trade opportunities using a risk-defined, institutional-style framework.

The system is not built around stock price alone. Instead, it focuses on structural conditions such as liquidity, float, participation, institutional positioning, and growth dynamics.

Price is treated as a contextual variable, not a filter.

=========================================================

CORE CONCEPT

Traditional retail screening focuses on:
- Stock price
- Simple technical indicators
- Static “cheap vs expensive” assumptions

This system focuses on:
- Float structure
- Liquidity expansion (RVOL)
- Institutional accumulation
- Revenue and EPS acceleration
- Short interest dynamics
- Catalyst strength
- Ownership shifts

A $0.80 stock and an $80 stock can behave identically if structural conditions align.

The goal is to identify **structural imbalance before repricing occurs**.

=========================================================

STEP 1 — FINDING CANDIDATE STOCKS (UNIVERSE CREATION)

Begin by building a broad watchlist of stocks that may exhibit institutional asymmetry behavior.

Do NOT filter strictly by price.

Instead, look for stocks that show:

- Increasing volume activity
- News or catalyst presence
- Unusual relative volume spikes
- Strong sector momentum
- Emerging earnings growth stories
- High short interest environments (optional)
- Low float or tightening supply conditions

Sources commonly used:
- Finviz screener (primary)
- Market news feeds
- Earnings calendars
- Sector momentum scans
- Unusual volume lists

The goal is to collect candidates — not finalize trades.

=========================================================

STEP 2 — STRUCTURAL SCREENING (INSTITUTIONAL FILTER LOGIC)

Once a candidate list is created, evaluate each stock using structural conditions:

Key structural metrics:

- Float size (critical for volatility behavior)
- Relative Volume (RVOL)
- Shares outstanding
- Revenue growth (Q/Q and YoY)
- EPS growth (Q/Q and YoY)
- Institutional ownership trends
- Insider transaction direction
- Debt-to-equity ratio
- Current ratio (liquidity health)
- Short float percentage

Interpretation principle:
Price alone is meaningless without structural context.

=========================================================

STEP 3 — PRICE AS A CONTEXTUAL SIGNAL

Price is not used as a filter.

Instead, it is interpreted within structure:

- Low price may indicate early accumulation or dilution risk
- Mid-range price often indicates institutional discovery phase
- Higher price may indicate leadership or mature trend structure

What matters is NOT price level, but:
- How price behaves relative to float
- How price reacts to volume expansion
- Whether price reflects improving fundamentals yet

=========================================================

STEP 4 — CLASSIFYING OPPORTUNITY TYPE

After screening, classify the stock into one of the following behavior zones:

1. Early Accumulation
- Low RVOL increasing
- Improving fundamentals
- Early institutional interest

2. Momentum Expansion
- Strong RVOL spike
- Catalyst present
- Rapid participation increase

3. Continuation Phase
- Stable RVOL
- Strong earnings growth
- Institutional trend support

4. Squeeze / Imbalance Phase
- High short float
- Low float supply
- Sudden RVOL expansion

=========================================================

STEP 5 — ENTRY INTO DASHBOARD

Once a candidate passes structural review:

1. Enter ticker into dashboard
2. Set account size and risk percentage
3. Define low and high price range (if applicable)
4. Allow system to calculate:
   - Risk per trade ($)
   - Stop loss level
   - Ladder entry prices
   - Position sizing per level
   - Total exposure
   - Buy-now execution model

The system will automatically structure risk and position sizing.

=========================================================

STEP 6 — ANALYSIS & JOURNALING

For each selected trade:

- Generate structured institutional analysis using the template system
- Attach trade notes and reasoning
- Review risk/reward structure
- Save entry into journal (CSV-based persistence)

Each entry becomes part of a historical trade database for review and refinement.

=========================================================

STEP 7 — ITERATIVE REVIEW

Over time:

- Review executed trades panel (e.g., Webull data import)
- Compare expected vs actual outcomes
- Adjust screening criteria based on performance
- Refine structural understanding of setups

This creates a feedback loop between:
screening → execution → review → refinement

=========================================================

KEY PRINCIPLE SUMMARY

This system is built on one core idea:

The best trades are not found by price.
They are found by structural imbalance.

Meaning:
- Liquidity expands before price fully adjusts
- Institutional positioning shifts before momentum becomes obvious
- Float constraints amplify movement potential
- Fundamentals validate sustained repricing
