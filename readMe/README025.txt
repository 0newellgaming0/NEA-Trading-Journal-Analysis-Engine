### Post 1.5 — Market Hours & Sessions (Pre-Market, RTH, After-Hours)

---

## Overview

The stock market operates in **distinct time sessions**, each with unique characteristics affecting **price action, liquidity, and trading behavior**. Professional traders consider these when applying **Wyckoff, Elliott Wave, and Fibonacci methodologies**.

- **Pre-Market:** Early session before regular trading hours; lower liquidity, often volatile  
- **Regular Trading Hours (RTH):** Main session, highest liquidity and participation  
- **After-Hours:** Post-market session; lower liquidity, increased volatility  

> Understanding market sessions is critical for **entry timing, risk management, and observing institutional behavior**.

---

## Market Sessions Explained

### Pre-Market (Typically 4:00–9:30 AM EST)
- **Liquidity:** Limited; price movements may be exaggerated  
- **Participants:** Mostly institutions, reacting to overnight news or earnings  
- **Wyckoff Lens:** Early accumulation or distribution can occur stealthily  
- **Elliott Lens:** Wave formation may begin here, often as prelude to RTH impulses  
- **Fibonacci Lens:** Retracements from prior session highs/lows can act as early reaction zones  

### Regular Trading Hours (9:30 AM–4:00 PM EST)
- **Liquidity:** Highest; most retail and institutional activity  
- **Price Action:** Clear trends, breakouts, and corrections  
- **Wyckoff Lens:** Markup and markdown phases are more pronounced  
- **Elliott Lens:** Major waves and corrections occur, with observable impulse and corrective structures  
- **Fibonacci Lens:** Common retracements and extensions are more reliable  

### After-Hours (4:00–8:00 PM EST)
- **Liquidity:** Low; gaps and sharp swings can occur  
- **Participants:** Primarily institutional, reacting to earnings and news releases  
- **Wyckoff Lens:** Post-close accumulation/distribution may set up the next day  
- **Elliott Lens:** Wave patterns may continue but are less predictable  
- **Fibonacci Lens:** Levels may provide targets for overnight movement, but reliability decreases  

---

## Why Market Hours Matter

1. **Liquidity & Volatility:** Determines risk and stop placement  
2. **Institutional Behavior:** Institutions often act outside retail focus  
3. **Wave Development:** Elliott impulses and corrections often initiate in RTH  
4. **Retracement Relevance:** Fibonacci levels are more significant during higher liquidity  

> The timing of trades within sessions can affect **risk/reward, probability, and overall performance**.

---

## Real-World Example

A healthcare stock reacting to an overnight FDA announcement:

- **Pre-Market:** Price jumps 5% on low liquidity; Wyckoff accumulation observed by institutions  
- **RTH:** Wave 1 impulse forms, retracing to 38.2% Fibonacci before continuation  
- **After-Hours:** Sharp moves as institutions adjust positions ahead of the next session  

> Knowledge of session dynamics allows professional traders to **anticipate reactions and structure trades accordingly**.

---

## Core Takeaway

> Market sessions are not just clock times — they influence **participation, price mechanics, and probability of moves**.  
> Effective trading combines session awareness with **Wyckoff, Elliott, and Fibonacci principles** for improved decision-making.

---

## Glossary

- **Pre-Market:** Trading session before RTH  
- **Regular Trading Hours (RTH):** Standard session with maximum liquidity  
- **After-Hours:** Post-market session with limited liquidity  
- **Liquidity:** Ability to execute trades with minimal price impact  
- **Wyckoff Accumulation / Distribution:** Institutional buying/selling phases  
- **Impulse Wave:** Directional move in Elliott Wave  
- **Corrective Wave:** Pullback in Elliott Wave  
- **Fibonacci Retracement:** Probable support/resistance level based on ratios  
- **Volatility:** Degree of price fluctuation
