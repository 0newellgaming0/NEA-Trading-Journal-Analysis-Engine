## OVERVIEW

The Kikkake module is a **liquidity trap reversal engine** designed to detect failed breakouts and failed breakdowns that immediately reverse back into the prior consolidation range.

It is based on the Japanese "Kikkake" concept:
> A breakout attempt that fails, traps liquidity, and reverses direction.

Unlike continuation systems (Mat Hold), Kikkake is a:
- ❌ Reversal-first structure
- ❌ Liquidity sweep detection system
- ❌ False breakout / false breakdown engine

It identifies:
- Stop hunts
- Liquidity grabs
- Failed momentum expansions
- Immediate structural rejections

---

## CORE CONCEPT

A Kikkake structure forms when:

### 1. Compression Phase (Inside Bar)
- Market enters tight consolidation
- Range contraction occurs
- Liquidity builds above and below structure

### 2. Liquidity Sweep (False Break)
- Price breaks above or below structure
- Stops are triggered (retail liquidity is taken)

### 3. Failure of Acceptance
- Price fails to hold breakout direction
- Rapid return into prior range

### 4. Reversal Confirmation
- Price closes back inside structure
- Opposite directional pressure emerges

---

## MARKET INTENT MODEL

Kikkake represents:

### Bullish Kikkake
- False downside breakdown
- Stops below structure are taken
- Price reclaims structure → bullish reversal

### Bearish Kikkake
- False upside breakout
- Stops above structure are taken
- Price rejects and falls back into range → bearish reversal

---

## EVENT LIFECYCLE

The system follows a strict event state machine:

### 1. SEED
- Early liquidity trap forming
- Inside bar present
- First directional probe detected

### 2. PENDING
- Full Kikkake structure formed
- Waiting for confirmation or failure

### 3. CONFIRMED
- False breakout validated
- Reversal direction confirmed

### 4. FAILED
- Breakout sustained beyond structure
- Trap invalidated

### 5. EXPIRED
- No resolution within timeframe (typically 15 bars)

---

## DETECTION LOGIC

### STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS

#### Inside Bar (Compression Core)
- `High(c2) < High(c1)`
- `Low(c2) > Low(c1)`

This defines:
- Liquidity compression zone
- Pre-breakout equilibrium

---

## FULL PATTERN DETECTOR

A full Kikkake requires:

### Bullish Kikkake (False Breakdown)
1. Bearish mother candle
2. Inside bar compression
3. False breakdown below inside bar low
4. Rapid reclaim into range
5. Close remains within structure

**Interpretation:**
- Downside liquidity sweep
- Immediate rejection
- Reversal upward pressure

---

### Bearish Kikkake (False Breakout)
1. Bullish mother candle
2. Inside bar compression
3. False breakout above inside bar high
4. Sharp rejection back into range
5. Close remains inside structure

**Interpretation:**
- Upside liquidity sweep
- Failed breakout
- Reversal downward pressure

---

## SEED DETECTOR (EARLY WARNING SYSTEM)

The seed detector identifies:

- Inside bar exists (compression confirmed)
- First liquidity probe begins
- No full breakout confirmation yet

### Bullish Seed:
- Downward probe into lows
- Immediate recovery back into range

### Bearish Seed:
- Upward probe into highs
- Immediate rejection back into range

---

## LIQUIDITY ENGINE BEHAVIOR

Kikkake is fundamentally a **liquidity hunting system**:

### What it detects:
- Stop-loss sweeps
- Fake breakouts
- Engineered liquidity grabs

### What it ignores:
- Clean continuation breaks
- Strong directional follow-through
- Sustained acceptance outside range

---

## EVENT RULE ENGINE

### SEED STATE
- Bullish:
  - Confirm if price breaks above structure
  - Fail if price breaks below structure

- Bearish:
  - Confirm if price breaks below structure
  - Fail if price breaks above structure

---

### PENDING STATE
- Same logic as SEED but with higher confidence requirement

---

### CONFIRMED STATE
- Only fails if structure is completely violated
  - Bullish fails if close < low
  - Bearish fails if close > high

---

## TRADE MODEL OUTPUT

Kikkake is a **reversal trading system**

---

### Bullish Kikkake Setup

- Entry: Break above structure high (reclaim)
- Stop: Below structure low (liquidity sweep low)
- Wick Stop: Inside sweep extreme
- Targets:
  - Target 1: Structure expansion
  - Target 2: Extended reversal continuation

---

### Bearish Kikkake Setup

- Entry: Break below structure low (breakdown continuation)
- Stop: Above structure high (liquidity sweep high)
- Wick Stop: Inside rejection extreme
- Targets:
  - Target 1: Breakdown extension
  - Target 2: Trend continuation lower
