EXPANSION PHASE ENGINE — README.TXT
Institutional Market Structure Detection Module
1. Overview

The Expansion Phase Engine is a structured market-regime detection module designed to identify true expansionary price behavior in OHLCV (Open, High, Low, Close, Volume) datasets.

It is built around a core institutional trading concept:

Expansion = Breakout + Volatility Increase + Volume Participation + Momentum Continuation

Unlike simple breakout detectors, this engine validates whether a move is structurally expanding, rather than being a false breakout or low-quality drift.

It is commonly used in:

Wyckoff-style phase modeling
Liquidity expansion detection
Trend continuation confirmation
Institutional momentum validation
Trade journal classification layers
2. Core Design Philosophy

This engine avoids naive indicators (like raw RSI or single moving averages) and instead focuses on:

Multi-factor structural confirmation:
Price structure (breakout of prior range)
Volatility regime shift
Volume expansion relative to prior equilibrium
Momentum quality (not just direction)
Trend alignment (continuation filter)

The goal is to detect when the market transitions from:

Compression / Accumulation → Expansion / Trend Initiation

6. Expansion Detection Components

The engine evaluates five independent institutional conditions.

6.1 Volatility Expansion
recent_range = mean(high - low over last 20 candles)
prior_range  = mean(high - low over previous 40 candles)

volatility_expanding = recent_range > prior_range * 1.30
Interpretation:
Measures range expansion vs prior regime
Detects volatility regime shift
Threshold:
Requires 30%+ increase in average candle range
6.2 Structural Breakout Confirmation
breakout = recent_high > prior_high

Where:

prior_high = max(high[60:-20])
recent_high = max(high[-20:])
Interpretation:
Confirms new structural highs
Validates market leaving prior resistance zone
6.3 Volume Expansion (Participation Filter)
volume_expanding = recent_vol > prior_vol * 1.25

Where:

recent_vol = mean(last 20 volumes)
prior_vol = mean(previous 40 volumes)
Interpretation:
Ensures breakout is participation-backed
Filters out low-liquidity fake breakouts
Threshold:
Requires 25%+ increase in average volume
6.4 Momentum Quality Filter
slope = np.polyfit(range(20), close[-20:], 1)[0]
volatility = np.std(close[-20:])

momentum = abs(slope) > volatility * 0.12
Interpretation:

This is NOT a simple trend check.

It measures:

Directional consistency
Strength relative to noise (volatility normalization)
Key Insight:
A steep slope in a noisy environment is required for expansion validity
6.5 Trend Continuation Filter
trend_continuation = slope > 0 and close[-1] > mean(close[-20:])
Interpretation:

Ensures:

Expansion is aligned with existing directional bias
Prevents counter-trend false expansions
7. Expansion Scoring Model

Each condition contributes to a weighted score:

Factor	Weight
Breakout	0.40
Volatility Expansion	0.25
Volume Expansion	0.20
Momentum Quality	0.10
Trend Continuation	0.05
Final Score:
confidence = min(score, 1.0)
Score Interpretation:
0.75+ → Strong expansion regime
0.45–0.74 → Transitional / developing expansion
< 0.45 → No expansion
8. Regime Classification Logic

🟡 TRANSITION STATE

Meaning:

Default fallback:

Market still in range-bound or non-expansion structure
Includes confidence inversion:
confidence = max(0.2, 1.0 - confidence)
