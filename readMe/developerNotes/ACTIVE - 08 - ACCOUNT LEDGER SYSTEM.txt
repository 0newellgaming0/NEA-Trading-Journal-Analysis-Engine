ACCOUNT LEDGER SYSTEM
VERSION: 1.0
AUTHOR: Newell Trading Group
MODULE TYPE: Investor Equity Accounting, Capital Tracking, Ownership Allocation, Profit & Distribution Ledger
FRAMEWORK: Python + Tkinter + CSV Persistence

=========================================================
OVERVIEW
========

The Account Ledger System is a complete investor accounting and equity management module designed to track:

• Investor Capital Contributions
• Investor Withdrawals
• Internal Transfers
• Trading Profit & Loss Adjustments
• Investor Distributions
• Fund Equity Growth
• Ownership Percentage Calculations
• Investor Net Equity Positions
• Investor Gain/Loss Attribution

The system functions as a lightweight fund administration and capital accounting engine.

Every transaction entered into the ledger automatically updates:

• Total Fund Equity
• Investor Capital Accounts
• Ownership Percentages
• Net Investor Equity
• Investor Gain/Loss Allocations

The module uses a persistent CSV-based accounting ledger and provides a graphical Tkinter interface for entry management, auditing, editing, filtering, and investor reporting.

=========================================================
PRIMARY PURPOSE
===============

This module was created to solve a common problem found in:

• Trading Partnerships
• Hedge Fund Simulations
• Family Investment Funds
• Proprietary Trading Firms
• Multi-Investor Accounts
• Private Capital Pools

Specifically:

How much does each investor own?

How much capital has each investor contributed?

How much has each investor withdrawn?

How much profit belongs to each investor?

What percentage of the fund does each investor currently own?

The system answers all of these questions automatically.

=========================================================
FIELD DEFINITIONS
=================

id

Unique transaction identifier.

Generated automatically using UUID.

Example:

A7F31C22

---

timestamp

Date and time transaction was recorded.

Example:

2026-06-17T14:53:10

---

investor

Investor name associated with transaction.

Examples:

John Smith
Fund
Partner A
Investor 01

---

entry_class

Internal accounting classification.

Possible values:

capital_in
capital_out
trade_pnl
distribution

---

transaction_type

User-facing transaction category.

Possible values:

Deposit
Withdrawal
Transfer
Adjustment

---

debit

Cash leaving the fund.

Examples:

Investor withdrawals
Transfers out

---

credit

Cash entering the fund.

Examples:

Capital contributions
Deposits

---

gain_loss

Profit or loss adjustment.

Used for:

Trading profits
Trading losses
Manual corrections

---

distribution

Cash distributed to investors.

Examples:

Quarterly distributions
Profit payouts

---

equity_after

Running account equity after transaction processing.

Automatically calculated.

---

ownership_pct

Investor ownership percentage.

Calculated dynamically by snapshot engine.

---

notes

Free-form transaction notes.

Examples:

Initial Funding
Q1 Distribution
Trading Profit Allocation

=========================================================
EQUITY CHAIN ENGINE
===================

Function:

rebuild_equity_chain()

Purpose:

Reconstructs account equity from the first transaction through the most recent transaction.

The ledger is treated as a chronological accounting journal.

=========================================================
EQUITY RULES
============

Capital Contribution

running_equity += credit

---

Capital Withdrawal

running_equity -= debit

---

Trading Profit/Loss

running_equity += gain_loss

---

Distribution

running_equity -= distribution

=========================================================
SUMMARY
=======

The Account Ledger System serves as the accounting backbone for a multi-investor trading operation. It maintains a permanent transaction journal, continuously calculates fund equity, allocates ownership percentages based on net capital contributions, attributes gains and losses proportionally, and provides a complete audit trail through a user-friendly Tkinter interface.

The result is a self-contained investor accounting and equity management system capable of tracking the complete financial lifecycle of a trading fund or investment partnership.
