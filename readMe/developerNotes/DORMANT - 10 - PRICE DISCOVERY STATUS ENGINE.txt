PRICE DISCOVERY STATUS ENGINE (INSTITUTIONAL REGIME CLASSIFIER)
MODULE OVERVIEW

This module implements a Price Discovery Status Engine, designed to classify market conditions into one of three structural regimes:

ACCEPTANCE
TRANSITION
DISCOVERY

Unlike phase-detection systems (Wyckoff phases, expansion phases, exhaustion phases, etc.), this engine does NOT attempt to predict lifecycle stages.

Instead, it functions strictly as a:
→ REGIME CLASSIFIER (macro structural market condition filter)

Its primary purpose is to determine whether price is:

Remaining within accepted value (equilibrium)
Transitioning between regimes
Actively breaking into new structural discovery (trend expansion / breakout behavior)
CORE DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

Markets are treated as an auction system:

ACCEPTANCE = Efficient value distribution (range-bound equilibrium)
TRANSITION = Early imbalance signals (uncertain structural shift)
DISCOVERY = Inefficient expansion (breakout + participation surge)

This avoids overfitting to classical “phase labeling” and instead focuses on:
→ structural behavior changes across price, volatility, and volume

Standardized dataset structure across modules
STRUCTURAL WINDOWING LOGIC

The engine splits data into two comparative regimes:

RECENT WINDOW:
last 20 candles → df[-20:]

PRIOR WINDOW:
previous 40 candles → df[-60:-20]

Purpose:
Compare recent structural behavior vs prior equilibrium

Fallback behavior:
If dataset is exactly 60 rows or slightly inconsistent,
windows are safely adjusted to prevent indexing failure.

FEATURE ENGINEERING LOGIC
PRICE STRUCTURE BREAKOUT DETECTION

recent_high = max(high in recent window)
prior_high = max(high in prior window)

recent_low = min(low in recent window)
prior_low = min(low in prior window)

Signals:

breakout_up → recent_high > prior_high
breakout_down → recent_low < prior_low

structure_break = breakout_up OR breakout_down

Interpretation:

Indicates structural inefficiency in prior accepted range
Primary driver of DISCOVERY classification
VOLATILITY EXPANSION REGIME

recent_range = mean(high - low) over last 20 bars
prior_range = mean(high - low) over prior 40 bars

volatility_expanding = recent_range > prior_range * 1.25

Interpretation:

Measures expansion of price displacement per candle
Filters out false breakouts with low participation
VOLUME EXPANSION REGIME

recent_vol = mean(volume over last 20 bars)
prior_vol = mean(volume over prior 40 bars)

volume_expanding = recent_vol > prior_vol * 1.20

Interpretation:

Confirms participation increase
Distinguishes real breakouts from low-liquidity spikes
TREND CONTEXT (SLOPE CONFIRMATION)

slope = linear regression slope of last 20 closes:

slope = np.polyfit(range(20), close[-20:], 1)[0]

trend condition:

trending = abs(slope) > std(close[-20:]) * 0.002

Interpretation:

Ensures directional movement has statistical structure
Filters sideways noise during volatility expansion
SCORING MODEL

Each condition contributes to a structural confidence score:

score = 0.0

0.45 → structure_break
0.25 → volatility_expanding
0.20 → volume_expanding
0.10 → trending

Maximum score is capped at 1.0

confidence = min(score, 1.0)

REGIME CLASSIFICATION LOGIC
🔴 1. DISCOVERY REGIME

Condition:
confidence >= 0.75

Meaning:

Strong structural breakout
Volatility expansion confirmed
Volume participation increasing
Directional trend present

Interpretation:
"Structural breakout regime detected. Price is leaving accepted value with volatility expansion and participation increase."

Use Case:

Early trend identification
Breakout confirmation
Momentum-based strategy activation
🟡 2. TRANSITION REGIME

Condition:
0.45 <= confidence < 0.75

Meaning:

Mixed signals
Partial breakout structure
Early expansion or failed breakout attempts

Interpretation:
"Market is transitioning between acceptance and expansion without confirmed breakout regime."

Use Case:

Caution zone
Position scaling
Watch for confirmation or rejection
🟢 3. ACCEPTANCE REGIME (DEFAULT)

Condition:
confidence < 0.45

Meaning:

Market is in equilibrium
No strong breakout structure
Range-bound or rotational behavior

Output adjustment:
confidence = max(0.20, 1.0 - confidence)

Interpretation:
"Market in equilibrium. No confirmed structural breakout or expansion conditions."

Use Case:

Mean reversion strategies
Range trading
Low-risk environments
