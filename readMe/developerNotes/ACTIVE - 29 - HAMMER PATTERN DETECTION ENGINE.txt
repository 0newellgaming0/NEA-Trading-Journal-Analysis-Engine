🧠 Hammer Candlestick Institutional Analysis Engine

A high-precision candlestick pattern detection and liquidity interpretation engine designed to identify, classify, and operationalize the Hammer candlestick pattern using institutional price-action logic.

This module treats Hammer candles not as isolated signals, but as:

Liquidity sweep → Rejection → Potential reversal or continuation depending on trend context

It is built for integration into trading journals, multi-timeframe dashboards, and systematic execution frameworks.

📌 Core Concept

A Hammer candle is defined as:

Small real body near the top of the candle range
Long lower wick (typically 2–3× body size)
Little to no upper wick
Appears after price decline or during support reactions
Institutional Interpretation:

A Hammer is not just a “bullish reversal candle.”

It represents:

Liquidity sweep below support
Stop-loss raid on weak longs
Aggressive rejection of lower prices
Potential absorption by buyers (demand emergence)

Hammer = Sell-side liquidity grab + potential bullish absorption

🧠 4. Hammer Detection Engine
detect_hammer(df, wick_ratio=2.0, body_threshold=0.35)

Identifies Hammer candles using structural liquidity rules.

📐 Definition Logic

A candle is classified as a Hammer if:

Conditions:
Lower wick ≥ wick_ratio × body size
Real body is small relative to range
Upper wick is minimal
Formalization:
body = abs(close - open)
range = high - low
lower_wick = min(open, close) - low
upper_wick = high - max(open, close)
Hammer Condition:
lower_wick >= wick_ratio * body
AND
body / range <= body_threshold
AND
upper_wick <= body * 0.5

The engine models Hammer candles as:

1. Liquidity Sweep Phase

2. Rejection Phase
