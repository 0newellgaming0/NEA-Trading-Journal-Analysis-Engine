Wyckoff Point & Figure Targets – Core Principles and Patterns

Overview
--------
The Wyckoff Point & Figure (P&F) methodology is a specialized technical analysis technique used to estimate price targets based on the Wyckoff Method’s price cycle principles. It combines the classical Wyckoff methodology of accumulation and distribution with Point & Figure charting to calculate objective price projections. This approach is widely used by professional traders and institutional analysts for high-probability trade setups.

Core Principles
---------------
1. Wyckoff Price Cycle
   - The Wyckoff Method divides market activity into four main phases:
     a. Accumulation (Phase A-D)
     b. Markup
     c. Distribution (Phase A-D)
     d. Markdown
   - P&F targets are applied primarily in the Accumulation and Distribution phases to estimate potential upside or downside.

2. Point & Figure Charting
   - P&F charts ignore time and focus solely on price movement.
   - Prices are plotted as columns of X’s (rising prices) and O’s (falling prices) in a grid format.
   - Box size and reversal criteria are key parameters in determining the sensitivity of the P&F chart.

3. Combining Wyckoff with P&F
   - Wyckoff events (e.g., springs, upthrusts) are identified on standard price charts.
   - P&F charts are then used to project price targets from these events using horizontal and vertical count techniques.
   - This allows traders to quantify potential moves objectively rather than relying on subjective projections.

Patterns and Techniques
-----------------------
1. Horizontal Count Method
   - Identify the width of the trading range during accumulation or distribution (measured in boxes on the P&F chart).
   - Multiply the width of the range by the box size.
   - Add (for accumulation) or subtract (for distribution) this value from the breakout point to obtain a target.
   - Example:
     Trading range width: 20 boxes
     Box size: $1
     Horizontal count target = 20 x $1 = $20
     Accumulation breakout price: $50 → Target = $50 + $20 = $70

2. Vertical Count Method
   - Measures the vertical distance from the support (accumulation) or resistance (distribution) line to the extreme of the range.
   - Multiply the vertical height by the P&F scaling factor (box size) to estimate the price objective.
   - Vertical counts tend to produce more aggressive targets and are often used for major trend projections.

3. Springs and Upthrusts
   - Springs: Sharp moves below support in accumulation phases followed by a quick reversal. Used as bullish P&F targets.
   - Upthrusts: Sharp moves above resistance in distribution phases followed by a reversal. Used as bearish P&F targets.
   - The magnitude of the P&F target is measured from the spring/upthrust to the opposite side of the trading range.

4. Wyckoff Event Mapping
   - Support, Resistance, and Trading Ranges are mapped onto the P&F chart.
   - Specific Wyckoff events (e.g., Preliminary Support, Selling Climax, Automatic Rally, Secondary Test, Sign of Strength, Last Point of Support) are marked for precision targeting.
   - Targets are calculated from breakout points of these events using horizontal or vertical counts.

Best Practices
--------------
- Always confirm P&F targets with Wyckoff phase analysis; do not use in isolation.
- Use multiple timeframes to refine targets (daily, weekly, monthly P&F charts).
- Account for volume patterns: high volume on breakout validates P&F targets.
- Monitor for failure tests or failed breakouts; adjust targets accordingly.
- Combine P&F targets with risk management: set stop-loss near the trading range extreme opposite to the breakout.

Advantages
----------
- Provides objective, mathematically-based price targets.
- Filters noise by ignoring time and focusing purely on price movement.
- Enhances Wyckoff analysis by converting qualitative patterns into quantitative projections.

Limitations
-----------
- Less effective in highly volatile or manipulated markets where ranges are distorted.
- Requires accurate identification of trading ranges; incorrect range selection will distort targets.
- Works best in well-defined Accumulation or Distribution phases.

Summary
-------
Wyckoff Point & Figure Targets provide traders with an objective methodology for projecting price movements based on classic Wyckoff principles. By integrating trading ranges, P&F charting, and Wyckoff events, this system converts market structure into quantifiable price objectives. Consistent application with proper risk management significantly improves probability-based trading outcomes.
